Dispute Resolution

COMPLAINTS AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION MECHANISMS

The Commission exercises its duties of investigating and determining disputes through the Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) framework. This is a quasi-judicial approach whose primary focus is mediation between/among feuding parties.

ENFORCEMENT OF DECISIONS OF THE COMMISSION

The decision/resolution of the Commission on Complaints or Dispute is final and applies to all persons affected by that decision. However, one has the right to apply for review of such decision/resolution at the High Court of Zimbabwe.

EFFECTS OF DISPUTES

  • Farmers spending time haggling instead of productivity,
  • Destruction of crops,
  • Vandalism of property and infrastructure,
  • Investor confidence eroded,
  • Security of tenure is affected.

DISPUTES EMANATE FROM:

  • overlapping boundaries,
  • double allocations on the same piece of land,
  • withdrawal of offer letters,
  • illegal allocation of land,
  • competing authorities over land allocation,
  • Farmer/Miner conflicts,
  • Land inheritance and divorce,
  • Conflicts on infrastructure sharing et cetera.

HOW TO LODGE A COMPLAINT OR DISPUTE CONCERNING AGRICULTURAL LAND

All complaints and disputes should be lodged at the ZLC offices in writing and addressed to either the Secretary or Chairperson of the Commission. For one to successfully lodge a complaint or a dispute, the following information and/or documentation is required:

  • There should be a major complainant.
  • Details of the other part with whom having dispute with especially phone number and home address.
  • Outline explaining the nature of dispute or complaint with emphasis on the extend of prejudice.
  • Date of occupation of the farm.
  • Date of issue of tenure document (permit for A1, offer letter for A2 or 99yr Lease).
  • Advise the Zimbabwe Land Commission on previous effort to find justice on that same dispute.
  • Copies of relevant tenure documents.
  • Complainant should fill in the Compliant Dispute Resolution Form (ZLC Form No. 1).
  • Provide details of witnesses available.
  • Provide 2 Copies of each of the document submitted.
