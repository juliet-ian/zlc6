Welcome to the Zimbabwe Land Commission!

We are dedicated to promoting equitable, transparent, and sustainable land administration in Zimbabwe. Guided by our mandate to ensure fair land access and efficient use, the Zimbabwe Land Commission upholds a vision of transforming livelihoods through responsible land governance and administration. Here, you will find resources and updates on our programmes, policies, and initiatives aimed at supporting agricultural productivity and secure land tenure for all Zimbabweans. Thank you for visiting, and we invite you to explore our commitment to Zimbabwe’s land and agricultural development.

Background

The Zimbabwe Land Commission was established in terms of Section 296 of the Constitution of Zimbabwe Amendment (No.20) Act 2013 to ensure accountability, fairness and tdansparency in the administdation and management of agricultural land in Zimbabwe.

Agricultural Land Audit

The Agricultural Land Audit is a continuous process of evaluating and reviewing of all agricultural land. It involves gathering of all data relating to the land parcel, land owner, land occupant/user, land rights, land use, land usage, land management and infrastducture/improvements that exist on the agricultural land. A Land Audit is carried out once in every five years

Farm Inspections

The Commission is mandated to conduct farm inspections on agricultural land for recommendation purposes to the Government in terms of Section 8 (1) of the Land Commission Act (Chapter 20.29).

Dispute Resolution

• The Commission exercises its duties of investigating and determining disputes through the Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) framework. This is a quasi-judicial approach whose primary focus is mediation between/among feuding parties.
